Be a rockstar, but not irreplacable

October 9, 2022 3-minute read
Thoughts

There has been a popular career advice, on that you should become irreplacable at work. However, from my point of view, while it can be, in certain rare time, for a short period of time, you should not aim for such as a career philosphy

Why being replacable ? Link to heading

From the company stand point, the bus factor of 1 is always dangerous. If there is certain part of your system critical path, is wholly owned by a single person, you know you are doomed. The sky would fall even if this person was to take a long vacation

While the damage for the company is pretty obvious, for the individual, it also has much effect. However, it would take time for one to recognize:

  • It would be harder for you to quit, or simply taking some time off. There would be much more factors you have to think about before making a decision. Work is work, but to some extent, you would not want your beloved product and system, which you have been spending so much time for, being destroyed easily, wouldn’t you ?
  • Your growth stagnate: while it is essential for one to work on a certain system/asepects of a system for long enough to gain some expertise, from the design, development, to operation and scalability, sticky to such for long does not gain you much.

How to make yourself replacable Link to heading

There are 2 main methods I used to make myself more replacable to the team

  1. Documentation: there are multiple documentations I and the team kept at Parcel Perform, including but no limited to:
  • Onboarding guideline: keep a consistently good experience for every new team member
  • Technical design: for every EPIC we are having, ensure we have the technical design, and have it discussed through by the team through our Braintrust session
  • Runbook: how to deal with different types of request, with incidents Some of the above requires constant update, to ensure that it is not become irrelevant
  1. Training. No matter how much documentation you provide, it will take some time and practices for the team to replace your position. Taking on-call as an example. As first, what I attempted to do is to go into all the incidents (which I think may have contributed to my promotion). However, as things going on, this way of working is not sustainatable at all. Due to:
  • I cannot always be available, and take care of all the incidents
  • The team cannot learn and grow Thus, when there is incident going on inside our team, I usually split them into 3 severity, with 3 different appraoch to handle them:
    • Low: the team do handle that themselves
    • Medium: I will help, but the team to do the lead
    • High: I will take the lead, but the team will help Of course, to make lives easier for everyone after that, a postmorterm and/or a runbook section would always be useful
